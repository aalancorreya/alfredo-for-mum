Chicken, Prawn, Mushroom & Bacon Alfredo

The pasta Stina asks for by name.

Serves 3-4 Prep 25 min Cook 25 min

Mum — this one is for you. I keep making it for Stina on the nights I want the kitchen to smell like a Sunday, and I wanted you to have it written down properly so you don't have to ask me again over the phone.

I made this the first time for Stina on a Wednesday night that wasn't meant to be anything special, and she asked for it again the next week, and the week after that. It is the dish that taught me you were right about browned butter — that you have to stand at the pan and watch it, that it doesn't forgive a wandering eye. Every time I cook it I think of you in our old kitchen, telling me to slow down.

For three to four serves

Ingredients

First, the proteins

The four pans

Chicken in the air fryer first; while it rests, bacon then mushrooms in the same skillet (the bacon fat does a lot of the work for the mushrooms); prawns last so they hit the table hot.

1

The chicken

Slice the breasts thin on the diagonal and toss with a tablespoon of olive oil, a teaspoon each of garlic powder and paprika, half a teaspoon of italian herbs, a teaspoon or two of lemon juice, and salt and pepper. Rest twenty to thirty minutes — two to four hours is better.

Air fryer at 190°C, single layer, ten to fourteen minutes, flipping halfway. Done at golden edges and 75°C internal.

2

The bacon

Medium heat, dry pan, no oil — the bacon has plenty. Five to eight minutes, stirring now and again, until the edges crisp and the fat renders into a glossy little pool. Lift onto paper towel and save a generous spoon of the rendered fat for the mushrooms.

3

The mushrooms

Into the same pan, in the saved bacon fat or a knob of butter, lay the sliced mushrooms in a single layer and — this is the part everyone gets wrong — leave them alone. Let them brown properly on one side before you stir. Add 1 clove of minced garlic right at the end so it perfumes the pan without burning, and finish with 1/2 tsp of lemon juice while the pan is still hot. Season, and set aside.

4

The prawns

Marinate with a tablespoon of olive oil, two cloves of finely chopped garlic, half a teaspoon of paprika, a pinch of chilli flakes if you like heat, a teaspoon of lemon juice, salt and pepper. Fifteen to twenty minutes only — any longer and the lemon starts to cure them.

Pan medium-high, oil or butter, single layer, one to two minutes a side. They are done the second they curl into a loose C-shape.

The prawns are done in ninety seconds — don't walk away.
A note from me — save a spoon of the bacon fat — the mushrooms cook in it.
Keep them warm A foil tent on the chicken, a low oven for the rest. Cold proteins drag the sauce down.
Reserve pasta water Half a cup, set aside before you drain. You'll want it at the toss.
Then, the sauce

The sauce, in four moves

Low to medium heat the entire way. Each move is short and forgiving as long as you don't let it boil hard.

i Roux

Two tablespoons of butter melted on low to medium, then two tablespoons of flour stirred in. One minute, smooth pale paste. Don't let it brown — this is the foundation for everything that comes after, so take the minute and watch it.

ii Milk

Two cups of milk, a splash at a time, whisking continuously. Patience gives you a glossy sauce. If it tightens, stream in more milk slowly.

iii Cream

Half to one cup of thickened cream, gentle low heat, stirred through. A soft simmer, never a rolling boil.

iv Cheese

One cup of freshly grated parmesan, a handful at a time, stirring each addition through until it melts. Salt and pepper to taste.

Bringing it together

Drain the pasta when it's just shy of al dente, keeping that half cup of water aside. Toss the bacon through the pasta first — tip the pasta into the warm pan with the bacon so every strand picks up the salt and fat. Then everything — chicken, prawns, mushrooms — goes into the sauce, and the bacon-laced pasta tips in after. Fold gently, and if it feels tight loosen with a splash of pasta water and another small splash of milk until it moves like silk. Serve straight away, in warm bowls.

A note on the lemon

three places, three small amounts

Lemon shows up in three places and each appearance is more restrained than you would think. One to two teaspoons in the chicken marinade. One teaspoon in the prawn marinade, and only for fifteen to twenty minutes. And, optionally, half a teaspoon to a teaspoon stirred through the finished sauce right at the end on low heat.

To finish, scatter a little lemon zest and chopped parsley over each bowl just before you bring them to the table.

a small warning Too much lemon, or boiling the sauce after the lemon goes in, will split the cream. Keep the heat low and the lemon small.
Zest first, juice second Zest the lemon before you cut it. Zesting a halved lemon is a small misery you can avoid.
Parsley Flat-leaf, chopped just before serving. Chop it any earlier and it bruises.

Notes

The imperatives

  • Salt the water like the sea.
  • Cook pasta al dente.
  • Reserve half a cup of pasta water.
  • Bacon through the pasta first; everything else through the sauce.

Texture rescue

If the sauce is too thick before the pasta goes in, stream more milk on low heat. If it tightens after — and it usually does — add a ladle of pasta water and a splash of milk and toss. Aim for smooth, silky, glossy.

To serve

Fresh parsley, a snowfall of extra parmesan, plenty of cracked black pepper, warm garlic bread, and a small lemon-dressed salad to cut through the richness.
